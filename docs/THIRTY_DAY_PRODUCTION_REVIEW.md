## Daily: days 1–7

- [ ] Review crash-free users, fatal/ANR regressions, and top Crashlytics issues.
- [ ] Review startup/network p95 traces and failed critical paths.
- [ ] Review App Check valid/invalid/unknown traffic without increasing enforcement prematurely.
- [ ] Review authentication, Functions, Firestore, Storage, Hosting, and FCM errors.
- [ ] Review AI accuracy feedback, p95 latency, error rate, and cost per success.
- [ ] Review Firebase spend and quota utilization against 80%/100% alerts.
- [ ] Record incidents, mitigations, Remote Config changes, and rollback readiness.

## Weekly: days 8–30

- [ ] Segment reliability by Android/Web version, device/browser, and release.
- [ ] Review AI expert disagreements and safety-critical false negatives.
- [ ] Verify backup completion and sample export metadata.
- [ ] Review pending/failed account deletions and retention jobs.
- [ ] Audit privileged access, service-account use, security logs, and dependency alerts.
- [ ] Review support themes and accessibility reports.
- [ ] Confirm alert primary/secondary ownership and escalation response.

## Day 30 decision

- [ ] Crash-free users meet the approved service objective.
- [ ] Performance and AI latency remain inside the approved envelope.
- [ ] No unresolved critical security, privacy, deletion, or data-loss issue exists.
- [ ] Cost forecasts and Firebase quotas support the next release window.
- [ ] Backup restoration and rollback evidence is current.
- [ ] Play staged rollout and Web compatibility evidence is complete.
- [ ] App Check enforcement stage is justified by legitimate-client metrics.
- [ ] Open incidents and accepted risks have owners and deadlines.
